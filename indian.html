Hyderabadi Biriyani
Originating in the kitchens of Nizams of Hyderabad,
it combines elements of Hyderabadi and Mughal cuisine.
Partially cooked rice is layered with fried onions, mint and half cooked meat
is sealed with dough and is slowly cooked in dum style to get that
authentic Mughlai flavor.

Malabar Biriyani
Made by layering an aromatic and herby chicken masala with rice,
crunchy fried onions, dry fruits and ghee in a
large pot. which is then cooked in steam. It holds a
special place in the hearts of Mallus.
